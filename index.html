Honest answers

Questions founders ask
before they join.

The Hive exists for founders at every stage — including idea stage. In fact, the earlier you join, the more value you extract. You'll shape your direction faster, avoid mistakes others have already made, and build relationships before you desperately need them. Traction is not a prerequisite. The right mindset is.

Most communities optimize for size. We optimize for signal. Every member is vetted. The network is kept deliberately small. There are no gurus selling courses, no engagement bait — just operators talking to operators about real problems. If it's not what you find in the first 30 days, we'll refund your first paid month, no questions asked.

Minimum viable engagement is one 45-minute call per month and a weekly digest. Connections are curated, not discovered through browsing. Most members report that Hive conversations replace, not add to, their weekly meeting load. You gain leverage; you don't lose time.

Every mentor commits to a minimum of 2 active hours per month. We remove mentors who don't show up — our members are too busy for ghost mentors. Response rate inside the Hive is above 85% within 48 hours, versus the ~8% you'll get cold-reaching LinkedIn profiles.

Agreed — that's why we have a free tier. Start there, get one match, attend one event. If it doesn't create more value than €25 in your first 30 days as a paid member, cancel and keep the connections you made. The cost of one bad strategic decision — the kind a good network prevents — is typically 6–12 months of runway. We're cheaper than one wrong hire.

We actively manage this. When you apply you declare your sector and geography. We do not onboard direct competitors. Founders in adjacent spaces enrich each other — directly competing for the same customer in the same market is something we screen for and prevent during the intake process.

A personal note

Dear founder,

I built Founders Hive because I spent two years building alone — and those were the two most expensive years of my life. Not in money (though there was plenty of that lost), but in time, in wrong turns, in opportunities I didn't know existed until it was too late.

The most successful founders I've met didn't figure it out by themselves. They had a room. A trusted group of people who'd been in the same trench — people who picked up the phone at midnight when the board meeting wasn't going well.

I wanted to build that room deliberately. Designed for depth, not reach. For operators, not spectators. For founders who are serious about making it — and honest about the fact that no one does it alone.

If that's you, you're already exactly who we built this for.

Justin H.
Founder, Founders Hive
